# Beyond the Job: Re-imagining Career Progression and Retention

Beyond the Job is a workforce systems transformation framework designed to unify recruitment, retention, employee development, and career progression into a single employee outcomes model. The initiative reframes workforce planning from a tactical hiring function into a broader organizational workforce and talent architecture strategy.

Built as an executive-oriented workforce strategy framework, the project explores how organizations can reduce fragmentation between talent acquisition, employee engagement, workforce planning, and retention efforts through integrated workforce systems thinking and organizational design principles.

## 🔍 Key Features

### Workforce Systems Transformation
Reframes recruitment, retention, and career development as interconnected organizational systems rather than isolated HR functions.

### Employee Outcomes Model
Introduces a unified framework focused on long-term employee progression, engagement, and workforce sustainability.

### Organizational Design Thinking
Explores how workforce structure, staffing strategy, and career architecture influence organizational effectiveness and retention outcomes.

### Strategic Workforce Planning
Positions workforce planning as an integrated business strategy function tied to organizational capability development and long-term staffing alignment.

### Executive Communication Framework
Designed as an executive-facing presentation artifact intended to support strategic workforce discussions, organizational planning, and talent architecture conversations.

## 🛠 Framework Methodology

Identify fragmentation points between recruitment, retention, and employee development processes.

Analyze workforce planning through a systems-oriented organizational lens.

Develop integrated workforce architecture concepts connecting employee progression with long-term organizational capability needs.

Translate workforce strategy concepts into executive-readable visual communication artifacts.
